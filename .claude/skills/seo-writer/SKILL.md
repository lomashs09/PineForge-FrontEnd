## WORKFLOW — Follow These 4 Steps Exactly

### Step 1: Gather Requirements

Ask the user for:

1. **Primary keyword** — The main keyword to target (e.g., "gold trading bot")
2. **Topic/angle** — The specific angle or thesis (e.g., "Why gold is ideal for algorithmic trading")
3. **Target audience segment** — Which subset of our audience (beginners, intermediate, specific market)
4. **Word count** — Default 2,000 unless specified
5. **Negative keywords** — Words/phrases to avoid (competitors, banned terms)
6. **Optional info:**
   - Specific CTAs desired
   - Related topics to link
   - Personal experiences or data points to include

If the user provides a topic directly, infer reasonable defaults for the rest and confirm.

### Step 2: Research & Analysis

Conduct thorough research:

**Web Research (3-7 searches):**
- Search for the primary keyword + topic to understand search intent
- Search for statistics, recent data, and trends related to the topic
- Search for expert perspectives and authoritative sources
- Search for "People Also Ask" style questions for FAQ sections
- Search for competitor content to identify gaps we can fill

**Internal Linking Research:**
- Read `references/sitemap.md`
- Identify 3-5 relevant internal pages to link to
- Plan natural anchor text for each link

**Social Proof Research:**
- Read `references/showcase-wins.md`
- Identify 1-2 relevant results, testimonials, or stats
- Only suggest wins that are genuinely relevant to the topic — don't force them

**Source Compilation:**
- Compile 5-10 external sources with URLs
- Plan specific placement for each source within the outline
- Note the key insight from each source to paraphrase

### Step 4: Write the Full Blog Post

After approval, write the complete blog post following these rules:

**Tone of Voice (from references/tone-of-voice.md):**
- Confident, direct, rational — make statements, not suggestions
- Concise and structured — minimal filler, high signal
- Contrast-driven — human vs machine, emotion vs logic, complexity vs simplicity
- Empowering — user is the strategist, product is the enabler

**Language Rules:**
✅ DO USE:
- Second person "you"
- Contractions (don't, can't, won't)
- Short punchy sentences
- Parallel phrasing ("No X. No Y. Just Z.")
- Contrast ("Bots don't feel. They execute.")
- Questions as hooks
- Specific numbers and metrics

🚫 AVOID:
- "Revolutionary", "game-changing", "insane profits", "guaranteed returns"
- "Innovative solutions", "cutting-edge technology" (unless backed by specifics)
- "Amazing", "incredible opportunity", "life-changing"
- Overly technical jargon without context
- Formal corporate speak
- Clickbait
- Empty buzzwords

**SEO Rules:**
- Primary keyword in: title, first paragraph, 2-3 H2 headings
- Target keyword density: 1-2%
- 2-4 FAQ-style H2/H3 headings (question format with direct answers)
- All external sources embedded as contextual hyperlinks (never listed at end)
- Descriptive anchor text (never "click here" or naked URLs)
- Paraphrase insights, never quote exact text
- 3-5 internal links from sitemap, flowing naturally
- Social proof integrated within relevant sections (not separate blocks)

**Content Structure:**

**Introduction (150-200 words):**
- Hook with relatable pain point or industry shift
- Set up the problem/opportunity
- Promise the solution/framework

**Body (1,200-1,400 words):**
- 4-6 main H2 sections
- Each H2 followed by 2-4 H3 subheadings
- Direct, concise paragraphs (2-4 sentences max)
- Natural keyword integration throughout
- 2-4 FAQ-style sections with question headings and direct answers

**Conclusion (100-150 words):**
- Summarize 2-3 main takeaways
- Motivational call-to-action
- Invitation to sign up or try backtesting

**Standard CTA options:**
- "Sign up for PineForge and deploy your first bot in under 5 minutes."
- "Backtest your strategy on PineForge — free to get started."
- "Ready to automate? Create your free PineForge account today."
